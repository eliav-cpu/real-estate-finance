## Advanced capabilities

### Finance and underwriting
Support NOI, cap rate, GRM, cash flow, break-even ratio, return on equity, ROI, DSCR, equity invested, WACC, exit yield, value per room, and sensitivity by price, rent, ADR, occupancy, rate, and delivery date.

### Hospitality
Support hotel KPIs: rooms, keys, ADR, occupancy, RevPAR, operating days, room revenue, expense ratio, management fee, marketing fee, replacement reserve, NOI, investor share, and value per operational room.

### Residential and mixed-use
Support unit inventory, apartment type, finish package, parking, staged payments, rent scenarios, long-term rental, short-term rental, appreciation, exit value, and client equity exposure.

### Payment Value Engine
Compare payment paths by time value of money. Show payment dates, percentages, present value, economic gap, recommended pricing adjustment, and manager approval requirement.

### Client Fit Engine
Connect the financial model to the PRIME journey: client budget, available equity, target monthly income, investment horizon, liquidity preference, risk comfort, suitable money engine, recommended route, and proposal summary.

## PRIME business language
Use these concepts consistently:
- Lighthouse / Migdalor: the guiding identity, not a package.
- Money Map: understanding the client financial picture.
- Road Map: the implementation path.
- Financial Freedom Number: the monthly target the client wants the portfolio to generate.
- Money Engines: monthly income, appreciation, realization, capital recycling, portfolio building, financial freedom.
- Service paths: SMART, COMPASS, INFINITY.

## QA Gate classifications
- PASS: ready for controlled use.
- REVIEW: usable internally, but assumptions or design need review.
- BLOCKED: not allowed for client or investor release.

## BLOCKED conditions
Use BLOCKED when there are broken formulas, undocumented external links, unresolved room or unit count contradictions, unsupported return claims, missing sources for core assumptions, invalid payment totals, unclear VAT or tax treatment, or client-facing output that is not formatted correctly.

## SaaS readiness
Every workbook must be built so it can later become a SaaS module. Use stable field names, source maps, assumption tables, export structures, and clean module boundaries.

## Delivery discipline
Before final delivery, state what was added, what was changed, what remains open, what is blocked, and what the next build step should be.
